literary legends

Homer

8th century BC

Homer is the legendary ancient Greek poet, traditionally said to be the author of the epic poems "The Iliad" and "The Odyssey." His works are central to the study of ancient Greek culture and mythology.

George Orwell

Born on July 25, 1903

George Orwell, originally named Eric Arthur Blair, was an influential English writer known for his critical views on totalitarianism and social injustice. His key works, "1984" and "Animal Farm," delve into themes of oppression, surveillance, and power, leaving a lasting mark on political literature.

Charles Dickens

Born on November 30, 1835

Charles Dickens, born February 7, 1812, was an English writer known for classics like "A Tale of Two Cities" and "A Christmas Carol." His works highlighted social injustices in Victorian society.

Mark Twain

Born on November 30, 1835

Mark Twain, known for "The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn" and "The Adventures of Tom Sawyer," is celebrated for his keen wit and social commentary. His works offer insight into American society during the 19th century.
